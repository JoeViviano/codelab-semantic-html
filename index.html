About Me

Hi everyone. My government name is Patience, but everyone these days calls me Soft Dog. Why do they call me Soft Dog? Well, I am not sure, but I am told that I have very soft fur. I am originally from Afghanistan, which is a tough place for a big softy like me. I used to get so hungry that I ate rocks. Luckily, I was rescued by a U.S. soilder who looked out for me! The soilder who rescued me was my first Person (I will explain Persons later), but he couldn't stay with me because of the war. I was so sad . . . until luck struck twice and my favorite Person in the world (Mary) rescued me and flew me to the U.S. Let me tell you, it was the best thing that ever happened! Now I live with my Persons Mary and Joe and I am so happy!!!!

Anyway, I am getting older now. I am around 8 years old, and through my years, I have learned a lot that I would like to share with my puppies who are still living in Afghanistan and any other dog out there just trying to figure out things in the U.S.! So, I asked my second favorite person in the world (Joe) to help me create this website. The rest of this website will tell you the most important things you need to know about living in the U.S.!

Your People

If I could only teach you one thing about living in the U.S., it is the importance of Persons. All the good things a dog could ever want --treats, pets, walks, and beds -- flow from his or her Persons. The bad news is that getting good Persons can be take time. It took me years on the streets before I found my Persons. The good news is that with hard work any dog can find good Persons. Just look at me!

Treats!!!!

I cannot over emphazize the importance of TREATS. I did not know about treats until I moved here, and let me tell you, they are AMAZING. I love treats. What is a treat? I am glad you asked. According to the Doggie Dictionary:

"A treat is any kind of really tasty food."
Almost all Person food is a treat. However, rocks, tomatoes, and peppers are not treats. Some dog foods are treats too. For example, there are things called "DINGOS" (D-I-N-G-Os) that are like heaven in your mouth. If you are in the U.S., you MUST get treats. A life without treats is a life not worth living.

So now that you know how good treats are, I am sure you want some, right? Of course you do. Just talking about treats makes me want a treat. Well, it's not that simple. Although treats are EVERYHERE in the U.S., you can't just get them whenever you want. But luckily for you, I am going to show you a sure fire way to great treats whenever you are in the house with your Persons.

Stay Away From Groucho!!!!

I have a lot of other things I want to teach you, but my Person Joe's plane ride is almost over and he said he needs to wrap this up. I want to tell you about things like beds, walks (and last but certainly not least pets), but I just don't have time. In the short time I have left, I MUST warn you about a very dangerous creature in the U.S. His name is Groucho and he is horrible. He complains all the time. One day Joe and Mary came back from a carnival with Groucho. I don't know how Groucho got in the car with them, but I suspect he snuck in because there is no way Mary and Joe would want him. Groucho is so miserable and angry. He stays in the closet all day and as soon as Mary and Joe leave in the morning to earn more treats for me Groucho starts screaming about how much he hates the closet and how he is going "get" everyone. I would love nothing more than to eat Groucho, but he sits on a high closet shelf that I can't reach. If you see Groucho, STAY AWAY FROM HIM.
